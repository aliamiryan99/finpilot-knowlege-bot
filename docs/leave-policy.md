# FinPilot Analytics — Leave and Remote Work Policy

## 1. Policy Overview

This document describes the fictional employee leave and remote work policy for FinPilot Analytics.

It is intended for employees, managers, HR staff, and internal knowledge systems. It explains annual leave, sick leave, personal leave, parental leave, public holidays, remote work expectations, and approval procedures.

This policy is fictional and created for a sample knowledge bot project. It should not be treated as legal employment advice.

## 2. General Leave Principles

FinPilot Analytics believes employees should have time to rest, recover, and handle personal responsibilities.

The company’s leave principles are:

1. Employees should plan leave responsibly.
2. Managers should support reasonable time-off requests.
3. Critical business coverage should be considered.
4. Health and family needs should be treated respectfully.
5. Leave records should be accurate and transparent.
6. Employees should not be expected to work while on approved leave except in rare emergency situations.

## 3. Annual Leave

Full-time employees receive **24 working days of annual leave per calendar year**.

Annual leave may be used for vacation, rest, travel, personal projects, or other personal reasons.

Annual leave is calculated on a calendar-year basis. Employees who join during the year receive a prorated amount based on their start date.

Part-time employees receive prorated annual leave based on their working schedule.

## 4. Annual Leave Approval

Annual leave must be approved by the employee’s direct manager.

Employees should submit annual leave requests at least **10 business days in advance** when possible.

For leave longer than 10 consecutive working days, employees should submit the request at least **20 business days in advance**.

Managers should review:

- Project deadlines
- Team coverage
- Customer commitments
- Existing leave requests
- Critical operational responsibilities

Managers should not reject leave without a clear business reason.

## 5. Carryover of Annual Leave

Employees may carry over up to **5 unused annual leave days** into the next calendar year.

Carried-over leave should normally be used by the end of the first quarter of the following year.

Unused leave above the carryover limit may expire unless local employment law or an individual employment agreement requires a different treatment.

## 6. Sick Leave

Employees may take sick leave when they are unable to work due to illness, injury, medical treatment, or recovery needs.

Full-time employees receive up to **10 paid sick leave days per calendar year**.

Part-time employees receive prorated sick leave.

Employees should notify their manager as soon as reasonably possible when they need sick leave.

## 7. Sick Leave Documentation

For sick leave of one or two days, employees normally do not need to provide medical documentation.

For sick leave longer than **3 consecutive working days**, HR may request reasonable medical documentation, depending on local law and privacy requirements.

Managers should not ask for unnecessary medical details. Employees are not required to disclose sensitive diagnosis information unless legally required.

## 8. Mental Health and Burnout

Sick leave may be used for mental health needs, burnout recovery, stress-related illness, or medical appointments related to mental health.

FinPilot encourages employees to raise workload concerns early. Managers should take burnout risk seriously and consider workload adjustment when appropriate.

## 9. Personal Leave

Employees may request personal leave for important personal matters that do not fit into annual leave or sick leave.

Examples include:

- Family emergency
- Moving home
- Urgent administrative appointment
- Important personal obligation
- Unexpected care responsibility

Personal leave may be paid or unpaid depending on the situation, local law, and manager or HR approval.

## 10. Bereavement Leave

Employees may take bereavement leave after the death of an immediate family member or close dependent.

FinPilot provides up to **5 paid working days** of bereavement leave for immediate family members.

Additional unpaid leave may be requested if more time is needed.

Managers should handle bereavement requests with sensitivity and should avoid unnecessary administrative burden during difficult circumstances.

## 11. Parental Leave

Parental leave depends on local law, employment agreement, and individual circumstances.

Employees expecting a child, adopting a child, or taking on significant caregiving responsibilities should contact HR as early as practical to discuss available leave options.

FinPilot supports planning for parental leave to ensure team coverage and a smooth transition.

## 12. Public Holidays

Employees are entitled to public holidays based on their employment location or contract terms.

When a public holiday falls on a normal working day, employees are not expected to work unless their role requires holiday coverage and this has been approved in advance.

If an employee must work on a public holiday due to business-critical needs, compensation or replacement time off should be handled according to local law and employment agreement.

## 13. Remote Work Policy

FinPilot Analytics supports hybrid and remote work depending on role, team needs, customer responsibilities, and security requirements.

Most knowledge work roles may work remotely by default, provided that the employee:

- Maintains reliable internet access
- Attends required meetings
- Protects company and customer information
- Uses approved devices and tools
- Remains reachable during agreed working hours
- Meets performance expectations
- Coordinates with their team

Remote work is a privilege and operational arrangement, not a reason to reduce communication or accountability.

## 14. Core Collaboration Hours

FinPilot uses core collaboration hours to make teamwork easier.

Standard core collaboration hours are:

**10:00 to 15:00 in the team’s primary timezone, Monday to Friday.**

Employees should normally be available for meetings and collaboration during core hours unless they are on approved leave, sick leave, or have a manager-approved schedule variation.

Outside core hours, employees may have flexibility depending on their role.

## 15. Working From Another Country

Employees who want to work temporarily from another country must request approval from their manager and HR before traveling.

International remote work may create legal, tax, payroll, security, or data protection issues.

Requests should include:

- Destination country
- Dates of travel
- Work schedule
- Whether customer data will be accessed
- Device and security arrangements
- Emergency contact information

Approval is not guaranteed.

## 16. Security Requirements for Remote Work

Employees working remotely must follow security requirements.

Employees must:

- Use company-approved devices when required
- Enable device encryption
- Use strong passwords
- Use multi-factor authentication
- Avoid public computers
- Avoid unsecured public Wi-Fi for sensitive work
- Keep customer data confidential
- Lock screens when away
- Report lost or stolen devices immediately
- Avoid discussing confidential matters in public spaces

Security concerns should be escalated to the Engineering or Security owner immediately.

## 17. Leave During Critical Projects

Employees may request leave during critical projects, but managers may need to coordinate timing to protect delivery commitments.

Managers should not automatically reject leave during busy periods. Instead, they should evaluate:

- Whether responsibilities can be covered
- Whether deadlines can be adjusted
- Whether another team member can temporarily own tasks
- Whether the leave is urgent or flexible
- Whether the employee has already delayed leave repeatedly

Health-related leave should be treated separately from ordinary planning concerns.

## 18. Unplanned Absence

An unplanned absence occurs when an employee cannot work and could not request leave in advance.

Examples include sudden illness, family emergency, accident, or urgent personal matter.

Employees should notify their manager as soon as reasonably possible. If the employee cannot notify the manager directly, they may ask a trusted colleague or family member to do so.

## 19. No-Show Absence

A no-show absence occurs when an employee misses work without notice and without a reasonable explanation.

Managers should first attempt to contact the employee and check whether there is an emergency. Repeated no-show absences may result in HR review.

## 20. Manager Responsibilities

Managers are responsible for:

- Reviewing leave requests fairly
- Maintaining team coverage
- Respecting employee privacy
- Avoiding unnecessary pressure during leave
- Recording approvals accurately
- Escalating complex cases to HR
- Planning handovers before long absences
- Supporting sustainable workloads

Managers should not ask employees to work during approved leave except in rare and urgent business-critical situations.

## 21. Employee Responsibilities

Employees are responsible for:

- Submitting leave requests on time when possible
- Communicating expected absence clearly
- Preparing handover notes for planned leave
- Updating calendars
- Notifying affected stakeholders
- Protecting confidential information while remote
- Returning company equipment if required during extended leave
- Following local rules and employment agreement requirements

## 22. Handover Before Leave

For planned leave longer than three working days, employees should prepare a simple handover note.

The handover note should include:

- Active tasks
- Important deadlines
- Key contacts
- Open risks
- Links to relevant documents
- Recommended backup owner
- Anything that should not wait until return

This is especially important for customer-facing roles, engineering releases, support coverage, and compliance-sensitive work.

## 23. Leave Request Process

The standard leave request process is:

1. Employee checks team calendar and project deadlines.
2. Employee submits leave request to manager.
3. Manager reviews business coverage.
4. Manager approves, rejects, or asks for adjustment.
5. Employee updates calendar after approval.
6. Employee prepares handover if needed.
7. HR or operations records leave where applicable.

Urgent sick leave and emergencies do not require advance approval, but employees should notify their manager as soon as practical.

## 24. Leave and Performance

Using approved leave should not negatively affect performance evaluation.

However, repeated unplanned absence without communication may require HR review if it affects work responsibilities.

Managers should distinguish between legitimate health or personal needs and avoidable communication problems.

## 25. Questions About Leave

Employees should ask their manager or HR if they are unsure how to classify a leave request.

For sensitive personal situations, employees may contact HR directly.

The internal knowledge bot may summarize this policy, but HR is responsible for final interpretation.
