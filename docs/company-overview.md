# FinPilot Analytics — Company Overview

## 1. Company Summary

FinPilot Analytics is a fictional financial technology and analytics company that helps small and mid-sized investment firms, trading desks, fintech startups, and financial advisory teams understand operational risk, evaluate client opportunities, and improve internal decision-making workflows.

The company’s main purpose is to make complex financial and operational information easier to analyze. FinPilot Analytics does this by combining structured business rules, natural language interfaces, data-driven scoring models, and human-in-the-loop review processes.

FinPilot Analytics does **not** execute trades, provide regulated investment advice, manage client funds, or make final financial decisions on behalf of customers. The company provides software tools that support analysis, documentation, risk review, and opportunity discovery.

## 2. Mission

FinPilot Analytics exists to help financial teams make faster, clearer, and more responsible decisions.

The company mission is:

> To turn scattered financial, operational, and client information into clear, explainable, and auditable insights for modern financial teams.

This mission reflects three internal principles:

1. **Clarity over complexity**
   Financial teams often work with fragmented information. FinPilot aims to organize that information into practical summaries, risk signals, and next-step recommendations.

2. **Human judgment remains central**
   FinPilot systems are designed to assist professionals, not replace them. Human review is required for any material business, investment, compliance, or trading decision.

3. **Traceability matters**
   Users should be able to understand why a system produced a recommendation, what source material was used, and where uncertainty remains.

## 3. Target Customers

FinPilot Analytics primarily serves organizations that work with financial data, client relationships, market research, trading operations, or investment analysis.

Typical customer profiles include:

- Boutique investment advisory firms
- Small hedge funds and trading teams
- Fintech startups
- Financial research groups
- Corporate finance teams
- Client success teams inside financial software companies
- Risk and compliance teams at small financial organizations
- Business development teams evaluating potential clients

FinPilot is especially useful for teams that have valuable internal knowledge spread across documents, spreadsheets, CRM notes, research memos, email summaries, and support tickets.

## 4. Core Business Areas

FinPilot Analytics focuses on four core business areas.

### 4.1 Client Audit and Opportunity Scoring

FinPilot helps teams review potential clients or accounts by analyzing available information and producing structured opportunity summaries.

The system can help identify:

- Whether a client is a strong fit for the company’s services
- Which products may be relevant to the client
- What operational risks or missing data should be reviewed
- Whether the client requires compliance escalation
- Which next action a sales, advisory, or support team should consider

Opportunity scoring is designed to support internal prioritization. It is not a guarantee of revenue, investment success, client suitability, or regulatory approval.

### 4.2 Knowledge Bot and Internal Search

FinPilot provides internal knowledge assistants that allow employees to ask questions about company policies, products, procedures, support rules, and compliance guidelines.

The knowledge bot is designed to answer only from approved internal documents. If information is missing, the bot should say that the knowledge base does not contain enough information.

### 4.3 Risk and Compliance Support

FinPilot helps teams organize risk-related information and detect situations that may require human review.

Examples include:

- A client asking for personalized investment advice
- A user requesting a trading recommendation
- A support ticket involving regulated claims
- A customer asking for performance guarantees
- Missing or inconsistent client documentation
- Unusual operational behavior that should be escalated

FinPilot does not make legal or compliance decisions. It surfaces signals and routes issues to responsible human reviewers.

### 4.4 Trading Operations Assistance

FinPilot can assist trading teams with workflow documentation, operational checklists, research organization, and post-trade review summaries.

However, FinPilot does not execute trades or automatically approve trading decisions. Any trading-related output is informational and must be reviewed by an authorized human professional.

## 5. Company Values

FinPilot Analytics operates according to five values.

### 5.1 Responsible Automation

Automation should reduce repetitive work and improve clarity, but it should not hide uncertainty. FinPilot prefers systems that clearly explain limitations instead of pretending to know everything.

### 5.2 Grounded Intelligence

AI-generated responses must be grounded in available source material. If the system does not have sufficient information, it should say so.

### 5.3 Auditability

Important outputs should be traceable to source documents, rules, or user-provided data. This is especially important in financial contexts where decisions may need to be reviewed later.

### 5.4 Practical Delivery

FinPilot values working systems over abstract concepts. Internal teams are encouraged to build small, testable features, evaluate them, and improve them incrementally.

### 5.5 Human Accountability

Humans remain responsible for final decisions. AI systems may assist with analysis, but they do not carry legal, fiduciary, or managerial responsibility.

## 7. Working Style

FinPilot Analytics uses a documentation-first working style. Important decisions should be recorded in project briefs, task files, implementation notes, or internal memory documents.

For AI-assisted projects, teams are expected to maintain:

- A clear project brief
- Context for future AI coding agents
- A memory log of decisions and mistakes
- A task list
- Evaluation cases

This working style helps future team members and AI tools understand why decisions were made and how the project should continue.

## 8. Communication Standards

Internal communication should be direct, respectful, and specific.

Employees are encouraged to:

- Mention assumptions clearly
- Separate facts from opinions
- Ask for clarification when requirements are ambiguous
- Document decisions that affect future work
- Raise risks early
- Avoid overclaiming certainty

For customer-facing communication, employees must avoid promising guaranteed financial results, guaranteed risk reduction, or guaranteed regulatory outcomes.

## 9. Support Hours

Standard support hours are Monday to Friday, 09:00 to 18:00 in the customer’s contracted business timezone.

Enterprise customers may receive extended support coverage depending on their contract. Extended support does not mean that all issues are solved immediately; it means that triage and escalation may be available outside standard support hours.

FinPilot does not provide 24/7 support by default.

## 10. Escalation Principles

An issue should be escalated when it involves:

- Possible legal or regulatory risk
- A customer asking for personalized financial advice
- A production outage affecting multiple customers
- Security or data privacy concerns
- Incorrect financial calculations in a customer-facing workflow
- A major client relationship risk
- A support request from an enterprise account marked as high priority

Escalation should include:

- Summary of the issue
- Customer or internal stakeholder affected
- Relevant source documents or logs
- Urgency level
- Recommended next action
- Owner responsible for follow-up

## 11. Product Philosophy

FinPilot products are designed around explainable assistance.

A good FinPilot product should:

- Make complex information easier to act on
- Show relevant sources when possible
- Avoid unsupported claims
- Help users understand uncertainty
- Provide structured next steps
- Keep human reviewers in control

The company avoids black-box automation for sensitive financial workflows.

## 13. Important Limitations

FinPilot Analytics has several important limitations:

- It does not provide investment advice.
- It does not execute trades.
- It does not guarantee investment returns.
- It does not replace compliance officers.
- It does not replace human review.
- It does not certify that a customer is legally compliant.
- It does not make final client approval decisions.
- It does not provide tax, legal, or accounting advice.

Any output from FinPilot systems should be treated as decision support, not final authority.

## 14. Internal AI Usage Policy Summary

FinPilot employees may use AI tools for drafting, coding assistance, summarization, testing, and internal analysis, but they must review outputs carefully.

Employees must not paste sensitive customer data into unauthorized AI systems. When using AI tools, employees should remove confidential information unless the tool has been approved for that data category.

For engineering work, AI-generated code must be reviewed by a human before being merged.

## 15. Company Knowledge Bot Expectations

The internal knowledge bot should answer questions using only approved documents. It should not invent policies, pricing, product features, employee benefits, or compliance rules.

When a user asks a question that is not covered by the documents, the bot should respond with a message similar to:

> I do not have enough information in the current knowledge base to answer that question.

The bot should also show which document sources were used when possible.
